Tactile Headband

Hackathon March 2026

First place out of 50+ teams in the Hardware track at Tech(HACK) Valley Hackathon 2026. Built in 36 hours, this wearable headband transforms 3D surround audio into real-time directional vibration feedback, enabling blind sound localization without relying on sight or hearing alone.

Details
  • Developed a Python script utilizing WASAPI loopback audio capture to intercept real-time surround sound output and transmit directional audio channel data over USB/UART with under 30 ms end-to-end latency.
  • Wrote C++ firmware on an ESP32 microcontroller to drive 8 directional vibration motors, mapping audio channel intensity to motor strength to allow users to localize sound within 22.5 degrees of resolution.
  • Sampled IMU (inertial measurement unit) data at 1125 Hz over SPI and applied a complementary filter to fuse accelerometer and gyroscope readings, mapping head-tilt angles to WASD keypresses sent over Bluetooth HID for fully hands-free directional control.
